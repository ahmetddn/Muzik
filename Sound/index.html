Drums

The drum is one of the oldest percussion instruments known. It is made of leather stretched from wood, metal or baked clay into a cylindrical body. It is played with hand or stick. Although its form changes, it is an instrument that is used in all parts of the world and in every society.


The drum is cylindrical and consists of binding of stretched leathers on either side of the wooden or metal pulley. It consists of a knob and a fine wand used to hit the shoulder with its slate. The drums played in the mehter and among the people are played with gavel and wand. The drums used in the band and pipe-trumpet sets are played with a mallet without a stick.

A piano is a key instrument. Sound on the piano is achieved through wires. When the keys of the piano are pressed, the wooden hammer inside it strikes the wires and creates the sound. Piano percussion musical instrument is believed to be struck by the wooden hammer.
